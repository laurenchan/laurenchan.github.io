Mind Games

Blind contour

With this exercise, students begin to transfer the technique of objective observation to a three dimensional object. A contour drawing is a drawing in which the eye follows along the edges of a three-dimensional object and the pencil, as with a pantograph, travels along the same path. In a blind contour drawing, the student looks only at the object and not at the drawing itself. For many, this is a very difficult thing to do because of a strong concern about how the drawing will come out. However, the blind contour drawing is the best exercise for effectively and efficiently causing students to later achieve good drawing skills because it forces them out of the "labeling" mode of thinking and thus allows them to begin to make accurate observations. Before they draw what they see, they have to learn to look at what they see. By looking only at the object being drawn without looking at the drawing itself, the mind is free to observe and appreciate the object’s complexity. The drawing itself becomes a record of a fulfilling activity rather than a product to be judged.
